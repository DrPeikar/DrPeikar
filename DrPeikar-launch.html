Dr. Mehdi Peikar, is an orthodontist and a physicist and a graduate of the UCLA School of Dentistry, is the inventor and founder of BRIUS. He also holds a Ph.D. in Biomechanics from Johns Hopkins University and a Master's degree in Quantum Mechanics and Condensed Matter from the University of Illinois. Utilizing his experience in biomechanics, Dr. Peikar engineered the BRIUS system, known also as Independent Mover or Brava, harnessing the latest in 3D printing, material science, and AI to deliver superior biomechanics for tooth movement. Following braces and aligners, BRIUS represents the third evolution in teeth movement techniques.

Dr. Peikar has secured over 60 patents and BRIUS Technologies Inc., the company he founded has raised more than $50 million in funding so far.

BRIUS

Brius Technologies Inc., founded by Dr. Mehdi Peikar, is at the forefront of revolutionizing orthodontic procedures with their innovative orthodontic device, the Brius or Brava. This unique device is part of a new category of orthodontic tools known as Independent Movers, conceived by Dr. Peikar, signifying the third generation of orthodontic treatment following traditional braces and aligners.

What sets the Brius apart is its unique design and the usage of a shape memory alloy called NiTi. Each tooth is connected to an anchorage bar via a flexible arm made from this innovative material. The flexibility and memory feature of NiTi allow the device to passively adapt to the alignment of the teeth and provide a consistent gentle force to guide them to their desired position.

The process of getting a Brius device begins with the orthodontist scanning the patient's teeth and sending the scan to Brius Technologies Inc. Using advanced software, the teeth are virtually aligned as per the orthodontist's instructions. After the treatment plan receives the orthodontist's approval, Brius, along with the brackets and digital bonding trays, are designed and manufactured. These components are then shipped back to the orthodontist for the procedure.

In the final step, the orthodontist bonds the brackets using the digital bonding tray and the arms of the Brius are engaged to the teeth. These arms are almost passive when the teeth are aligned. When the arms are stretched or squeezed to engage with the teeth, they apply a precise force/moment that guides the teeth to their ideal final position. Through this process, Brius provides an efficient and technologically advanced solution for aligning teeth.
